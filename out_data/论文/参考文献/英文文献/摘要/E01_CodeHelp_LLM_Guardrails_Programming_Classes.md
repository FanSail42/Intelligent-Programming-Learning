## 摘要

Computing educators face significant challenges in providing timely support to students, especially in large class settings. Large language models (LLMs) show great promise for on-demand help at scale, but there are concerns that students may over-rely on model outputs. This paper introduces CodeHelp, an LLM-powered tool with guardrails to provide on-demand assistance without directly revealing solutions. The authors deployed it in a first-year course with 52 students over 12 weeks and report usage patterns, student perceptions, and instructor reflections.
